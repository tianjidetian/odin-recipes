Eggplant Parmigiana

Description

Think of Eggplant Parmigiana as a pasta-less vegetarian Lasagna, made with thin slices of eggplant instead of pasta, layered with tomato sauce, fresh basil and plenty of molten cheese. Called Parmigiana di Melanzane in Italian and known as Eggplant Parmesan in the US, it’s a low-carb dream come true!

A southern Italian classic, this is one of those incredible vegetarian dishes that’s so good, even card-carrying carnivores won’t pause for a moment to wonder, “Where’s the meat??” This is comfort food for everyone. It oozes with cheesy goodness – molten mozzarella on top and the savoury hum of parmesan within. It’s slick and bright with fresh tomato sauce. It’s dotted with addictive pops of fresh basil leaves like a great margherita pizza. And with every bite, you get a mouthful of the juicy, soft eggplant layers.

Ingredients

  • Eggplants
  • Olive oil
  • Tomato puree
  • Crushed or finely diced tomato
  • Oregano
  • Garlic
  • Sugar
  • Parmesan
  • Mozzarella
  • Fresh basil

Steps

  1. Slice the eggplant lengthwise into 8mm thick slices. Place on to three lined trays and brush each side of every slice with olive oil.
  2. Bake in a 220°C (430°F) oven for 35 to 40 minutes until the eggplant is lightly browned.
  3. Sauté onion and garlic with the oil, then add everything else (tomato passata, crushed tomato, oregano or basil, salt and pepper).
  4. Simmer for 30 minutes until it reduces into a fairly thick sauce, as pictured below. You should be able to draw a path along the base of the pot that remains visible for a few seconds.
  5. Smear some tomato sauce on the base of a baking dish. This stops the first layer of eggplant from sliding around.
  6. Top with a layer of eggplant, using 1/3 of the eggplant.
  7. Spread eggplant with 1/3 of the tomato sauce.
  8. Drizzle with a little olive oil.
  9. Scatter with 1/3 of the basil leaves.
  10. Sprinkle with 1/3 of the parmesan.
  11. Repeat layering twice more for a total of three eggplant layers. With the top-most layer, (a) add everything except the basil, which we’ll save it for fresh garnish later; (b) sprinkle over all the mozzarella for our melted cheesy topping!
  12. Bake for 25 minutes in a 180°C (350°F) oven until the mozzarella is bubbly and golden, and the smells wafting out of your oven are knee-bucklingly good!
